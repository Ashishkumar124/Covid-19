How Virus Spread

How Contagion Coronavirus

COVID-19 spreads when an infected person breathes out droplets and very small particles that contain the virus. These droplets and particles can be breathed in by other people or land on their eyes, noses, or mouth. In some circumstances, they may contaminate surfaces they touch.

  • Human Contact

    People can catch COVID-19 from others who have the virus. The disease can spread from person to person through small droplets from the nose or mouth which are spread when a person with COVID-19 coughs or exhales. These droplets land on objects and surfaces around the person.

  • Air Transmission

    When someone coughs or sneezes they spray small liquid droplets from their nose or mouth which may contain virus. If you are too close, you can breathe in the droplets, including the COVID-19 virus if the person coughing has the disease. Avoid touching eyes, nose and mouth.

  • Contaminated Objects

    These droplets land on objects and surfaces around the person. Other people then catch COVID-19 by touching these objects or surfaces, then touching their eyes, nose or mouth. People can also catch COVID-19 if they breathe in droplets from a person with COVID-19 who coughs out or exhales droplets.

Corona Virus Symptoms

People with COVID-19 have reported a wide range of symptoms ranging from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Anyone can have mild to severe symptoms. Possible symptoms include:

  • Coughing And Sneezing

    The virus infects cells in the respiratory tract, causing irritation and inflammation. This triggers the body's cough reflex as it attempts to clear the airways.

  • Hot Fever

    A hot fever is a common symptom of COVID-19. It is characterized by a body temperature of 100.4 °F (38 °C) or higher.

  • Strong Headacke

    A strong headache is a common symptom experienced by many COVID-19 patients. It can be persistent and intense, often described as a severe pressure or throbbing sensation.

  • Shortness Of Breath

    The virus can cause inflammation in the lungs and airways, leading to reduced oxygen intake and respiratory distress. Severe cases can result in pneumonia or acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS).

  • Confusion

    Confusion, also known as delirium, is a state of mental disorientation where a person may have difficulty thinking clearly, remembering, or making decisions.

  • Sore Throat

    The virus can infect and cause inflammation in the tissues of the throat and upper respiratory tract, leading to discomfort and soreness.

Prevention Coronavirus

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends a COVID-19 vaccine for everyone age 6 months and older. The COVID-19 vaccine can lower the risk of death or serious illness caused by COVID-19.

  • Wash Your Hands For 20sec

    Wash your hands well and often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.

  • Cover Nose and Mouth When Sneezing

    Cough or sneeze into a tissue or your elbow. Then wash your hands.

  • Wear A Mask If Available

    The CDC recommends that people wear a mask in indoor public spaces if you're in an area with a high number of people with COVID-19 in the hospital. They suggest wearing the most protective mask possible that you'll wear regularly, that fits well and is comfortable.
